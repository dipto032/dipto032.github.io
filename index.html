Dipobrato Sarbapalli

I am a Ph.D. student in Materials Science and Engineering at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign working with Prof. Joaquín Rodríguez-López. My Ph.D. research focuses broadly on electrochemical energy storage systems, such as lithium-ion and redox flow batteries. I am trained as an experimental materials scientist, and I actively try to utilize Python and Matlab scripts for efficient data analysis with an ultimate motive of learning skills such as Machine Learning.

Prior to this, I graduated with a MS in Civil Engineering from UIUC in 2018, where I worked with Professor Paramita Mondal (currently at University of Delaware) on studying reaction mechanisms in inorganic binder materials termed as geopolymers. I spent the summer of 2017 working as a DAAD Fellow with BASF at their headquarters in Ludwigshafen, where I worked with Dr. Tobias Umbach. I received my B.Tech in Civil Engineering from NIT Trichy, graduating first class with honors. During my undergraduate studies, I spent my sophomore and junior year summers interning at the IIT Delhi with Dr.Biswajit Bhattacharjee, researching on durability and sustainability of concrete binder materials.

Research

I'm interested in solving fundamental problems in Applied Math and Numerical Analysis. A large component of my research spans around discretizing PDEs using the Finite Element Method, and more often than not, using the FEniCS project. In the past, I have also done numerical homogenization calculations in Abaqus (using UEL, UMAT, UHYPER,...), though lately my focus has moved to FEniCS and Firedrake.
