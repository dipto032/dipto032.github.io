Bhavesh Shrimali

I am a Ph.D. student in Materials Science and Engineering at UIUC working with Prof. Joaquin Rodriguez-Lopez/a>. My primary research area is theoretical and computational mechanics, where I work in the broad domain of analytical and numerical homogenization. This involves studying partial differential equations (PDEs) whose coefficients oscillate at multiple (largely different) scales. A natural manifestation of such PDEs occurs in highly heterogeneous materials such as composites. Currently I am working on the homogenization of porous/perforated composites (see our recent paper below). I am also pursuing a minor in Computational Science and Engineering

Prior to this, I graduated with a MS in Civil Engineering (with a minor in CSE) from UIUC in 2017. Earlier, I completed my B.Tech in Civil Engineering from IIT Guwahati, ranking first in the graduating class. During that time I spent a wonderful summer at TU Braunschweig (2014) as a DAAD-WISE fellow. I also spent an equally awesome summer at (HKUST 2013) working with Prof Chris Leung.

Research

I'm interested in solving fundamental problems in Applied Math and Numerical Analysis. A large component of my research spans around discretizing PDEs using the Finite Element Method, and more often than not, using the FEniCS project. In the past, I have also done numerical homogenization calculations in Abaqus (using UEL, UMAT, UHYPER,...), though lately my focus has moved to FEniCS and Firedrake.
